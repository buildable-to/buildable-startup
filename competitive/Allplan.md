# Allplan Precast — Competitive Teardown

*Status: evaluated (2-hour demo call with reseller, 3/04/2026)*

## Overview
- **Owner:** Nemetschek Group (Frankfurt: NEM.DE), market cap ~€7.2B
- **Revenue:**  €1.19B (2025), growing ~20% YoY
- **Approach:** Parametric BIM-first, 3D model as source of truth → auto-generated 2D drawings
- **Target:** Large precast plants and structural engineering firms, primarily DACH/Europe
- **Pricing:** ~€7,400–8,500/seat/year (subscription)

## What They Do Well
- Fully parametric 3D model with automatic 2D drawing generation (10–20 drawings/day after full onboarding)
- 2D drawings are bi-directionally synced with 3D model — change one, both update automatically
- Deep precast specialization: corbels, reinforcement, production data, ERP/MES/CNC integration
- Large installed base and battle-tested for high-volume repetitive precast work

## What They Do Poorly
- Extremely heavy onboarding: 2 months + 1 week before engineers are fully productive
- Requires a dedicated internal "key user" coordinator — a sign the tool is too complex for normal adoption
- No AI anywhere in the core workflow — pure parametric modeling
- Engineers must build a 3D element library from scratch per project type before automation kicks in
- Completely changes how engineers work — not additive to existing workflows
- Could not provide concrete speed/time savings numbers — reseller spoke mostly theoretically

## Workflow Summary
1. Build 3D element library (done with Allplan engineer embedded in your team during first project)
2. Engineer does all detailing/reinforcement/calculation work in 3D model
3. 2D shop drawings auto-generated from 3D model
4. Changes synced bidirectionally between 3D and 2D

## Speed Benchmark
- Allplan: 10–20 drawings/day (after full onboarding + library built)
- Manual (Nika baseline): 5–10 drawings/day
- Net Allplan gain: ~2× — but only after 2 months+ investment and full workflow change

## Pricing
Subscription based model: ~€7,400–8,500/seat/year

## Could They Pivot to AI Text-to-CAD?
Unlikely short-term. Their moat is the installed base of parametric BIM workflows. Moving to prompt-based generation would cannibalize their core product and alienate existing customers. They are adding AI for validation and checking (Precast Data Validator, Firmus AI acquisition) — not for generation.

## Acquisition / Partnership Risk
High. Nemetschek acquired Bluebeam (2014, $100M, ~4.5× revenue) and Firmus AI (2025) to fill workflow gaps. If Buildable reaches meaningful ARR and adoption, acquisition is a realistic scenario. This is both an opportunity and a threat.

## Key Takeaway
Allplan is the gold standard in precast BIM — powerful, deeply integrated, and battle-tested. But it is old, has no AI, and demands a massive commitment from customers. Buildable's real advantage is not beating Allplan on features — it's being applicable to how engineers already work, from day one, with no onboarding tax. The middle ground between Allplan's depth and Buildable's speed is exactly where the product should evolve.
